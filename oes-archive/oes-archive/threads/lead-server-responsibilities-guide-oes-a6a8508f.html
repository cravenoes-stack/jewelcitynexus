Here’s a sample job expectations and responsibilities document for the Lead Server position, along with some suggestions tailored to your restaurant, The Cellar Door: --- **Position Title:** Lead Server **Location:** The Cellar Door, 905 3rd Avenue, Huntington, WV **Reports To:** Restaurant Manager/Owner ### **Job Overview:** The Lead Server at The Cellar Door will play a critical role in ensuring our guests have an exceptional dining and bar experience. This position requires strong leadership skills, excellent customer service, and a deep understanding of our menu offerings, including our signature cocktails. The Lead Server will guide and support the serving staff while maintaining a high standard of service throughout all guest interactions. ### **Key Responsibilities:** **1. Leadership and Training:** - Train and mentor new and existing servers, ensuring they understand our standards of service, menu offerings, and upselling techniques. - Lead by example during service, demonstrating exceptional customer service, efficiency, and professionalism. - Conduct regular training sessions on service procedures, menu knowledge, and customer interaction, ensuring the team is well-prepared for any situation. **2. Customer Service:** - Greet and seat guests in a warm and welcoming manner. - Handle guest complaints or concerns efficiently, escalating to management when necessary. - Ensure that all guests receive prompt, courteous, and accurate service. - Maintain a strong floor presence, interacting with guests to enhance their experience and gather feedback. **3. Order Management and Coordination:** - Oversee the accuracy of orders taken by servers, ensuring they are promptly delivered to guests. - Communicate effectively with the barback and bartenders to ensure smooth service, especially during peak hours. - Coordinate with kitchen staff (if applicable) to ensure that food orders are delivered according to guest preferences and dietary requirements. **4. Inventory and Stock Management:** - Assist with the monitoring and management of inventory levels for server-related supplies (e.g., silverware, glassware, napkins). - Ensure that the serving stations are fully stocked and organized before and during service. **5. Sales and Upselling:** - Promote The Cellar Door’s specials, signature cocktails, and high-margin items to increase overall sales. - Train servers on effective upselling techniques and monitor their performance to ensure targets are met. **6. Compliance and Safety:** - Ensure all servers adhere to health and safety regulations, including proper food handling and alcohol service protocols. - Enforce cleanliness standards in the dining and bar areas, as well as in serving stations. - Maintain up-to-date knowledge of local and state regulations related to food service and alcohol sales. **7. Administrative Duties:** - Assist the Restaurant Manager with scheduling, ensuring adequate staff coverage during peak hours. - Complete end-of-shift reports, noting any incidents, guest feedback, or areas for improvement. - Participate in weekly meetings with management to discuss performance, challenges, and opportunities for growth. **8. Event and Promotion Support:** - Support the execution of special events, promotions, and themed nights at The Cellar Door. - Work closely with the management team to develop and implement strategies to improve service quality and guest satisfaction. ### **Qualifications:** - Proven experience as a server, with a minimum of 2 years in a high-volume or upscale dining environment. - Strong leadership and communication skills. - In-depth knowledge of food, wine, and cocktail service. - Ability to handle difficult situations with tact and professionalism. - Must be able to work evenings, weekends, and holidays as needed. - Certification in responsible alcohol service (e.g., TIPS) is preferred. ### **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):** - Customer satisfaction scores (e.g., from surveys or online reviews). - Sales performance, including upselling metrics. - Team performance and cohesion (e.g., through feedback and observation). - Efficiency in service, measured by table turnover rates and order accuracy. ### **Physical Requirements:** - Ability to stand and walk for extended periods. - Ability to lift and carry trays, dishes, and other items up to 25 pounds. --- ### **Suggestions for Customization:** 1. **Training Focus:** Given your emphasis on signature cocktails and unique offerings at The Cellar Door, ensure the Lead Server is well-versed in mixology basics and can train others on the nuances of your signature drinks. 2. **Upselling and Specials:** Make the promotion of specials and signature items a significant part of the Lead Server's role, perhaps including a small incentive for meeting sales goals. 3. **Community Engagement:** Since Huntington is a smaller city, the Lead Server could also play a role in community engagement, helping to foster relationships with regulars and local influencers. 4. **Feedback Mechanism:** Incorporate a system where the Lead Server collects and reports guest feedback to management, which could be vital for fine-tuning the guest experience. This document sets a clear expectation for the Lead Server role while allowing some flexibility for your specific needs at The Cellar Door.
