### Pricing Alcohol in a Bar Without Kitchen Labor Costs Since you are transitioning to a bar model with food prepared by barbacks, eliminating kitchen labor costs can allow for some flexibility in your pricing strategy. Here‚Äôs a guide to help you price your alcohol: 1. **Understand Your Costs**: - **Cost of Goods Sold (COGS)**: Calculate the actual cost of each drink, including ingredients, garnishes, and any associated costs. - **Fixed Costs**: Include rent, utilities, and salaries for staff, including barbacks. - **Variable Costs**: Consider costs that change based on the volume of business, like bar supplies and cleaning. 2. **Industry Standard Pricing**: - Typically, bars aim for a pour cost (the cost of the alcohol in a drink) between 18-24%. This means if a drink costs you $1 to make, you should price it between $4.17 to $5.56. - Higher-end establishments might aim for a lower pour cost percentage, reflecting their upscale nature. 3. **Competitive Analysis**: - Research prices at similar upscale bars in your area. Ensure your prices are competitive but reflective of your upscale environment and the quality of service. 4. **Menu Engineering**: - **Signature Cocktails**: These can be priced higher due to their unique recipes and presentation. - **Classic Cocktails**: These should be priced in line with industry standards, considering your upscale positioning. - **Premium and House Brands**: Price premium brands higher to reflect their quality and desirability. House brands can be priced lower to offer value options. 5. **Bundling and Specials**: - **Happy Hour**: Offer discounts during specific times to attract more customers. - **Combos**: Bundle drinks with food items prepared by the barback for a special price. 6. **Dynamic Pricing**: - Adjust prices based on demand, time of day, and special events. For example, prices might be higher on weekends or during live events. ### Sample Pricing Strategy 1. **Beer**: - Domestic: $5 - $6 - Imported: $7 - $9 - Craft: $8 - $12 2. **Wine**: - House Wine: $8 - $10 per glass - Premium Wine: $12 - $18 per glass 3. **Spirits**: - House Brands: $7 - $9 per drink - Call Brands: $9 - $12 per drink - Premium Brands: $12 - $18 per drink 4. **Cocktails**: - Classic Cocktails: $10 - $14 - Signature Cocktails: $15 - $20 By following these guidelines, you can create a pricing model that reflects your upscale bar concept while ensuring profitability.
